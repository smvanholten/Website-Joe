Bio

I am a macroeconomist at the London School of Economics. My research studies inflation, wage dynamics, monetary and fiscal policy, and household economics. I am particularly interested in combining new microdata with macroeconomic theory to understand how prices and wages are set, and how households and firms respond to policy.

I am affiliated with the Centre for Macroeconomics, the Centre for Economic Policy Research (CEPR), and the Institute of Labor Economics (IZA). I serve as Associate Editor at the Journal of the European Economic Association and as a member of the editorial board of the Review of Economic Studies.

Prior to joining LSE I was a postdoctoral fellow at Princeton University. I received my PhD from MIT in 2020 and my undergraduate degree from the University of Cambridge in 2015.
